This Notice tells you how Novara AI uses your personal data when an organisation uses our TalentLens service to assess your job application. We've tried to write it in plain language. If anything is unclear, please contact us using the details at the end of this Notice.

---

## Who we are

This Notice is provided by **Novara AI, Inc.** ("Novara", "we", "us"), a company headquartered in Dublin, Ireland. We provide AI-powered services to enterprise organisations.

The organisation reviewing your application (your prospective employer or their recruitment agency — referred to here as the "Hiring Organisation") uses our **TalentLens** service to help review and rank candidate applications. This Notice covers Novara's role in that process.

For information about how the Hiring Organisation handles your data more broadly (beyond their use of TalentLens), please see the Hiring Organisation's own privacy notice or contact their HR or recruitment team.

---

## What we do with your data

When the Hiring Organisation uses TalentLens to review your application, the following happens:

1. **The Hiring Organisation submits your CV to TalentLens** along with the job description for the role you've applied for.

2. **TalentLens processes your CV using artificial intelligence.** Specifically, we use a fine-tuned large language model called NovaraNet-HR-v2.3 to assess how well your skills and experience align with the role requirements.

3. **TalentLens produces a structured assessment** that includes: skills identified in your CV; alignment with the role requirements; identified strengths and gaps; a relative ranking when you are assessed alongside other candidates.

4. **The assessment is returned to the Hiring Organisation's recruiter.** The recruiter at the Hiring Organisation reviews the assessment as part of their broader review of your application.

We process the following data about you:

- The content of your CV (work history, education, skills, contact details)
- The job description against which you are being assessed
- AI-generated assessment of your skill alignment with the role

We do **not**:
- Use your CV to train Novara's AI models without an explicit Data Processing Agreement opt-in from your Hiring Organisation
- Share your data with third parties beyond what is required to operate the service
- Make final hiring decisions on behalf of the Hiring Organisation; the Hiring Organisation's recruiter retains decision-making authority

---

## Why we do it

We process your data on behalf of the Hiring Organisation so that they can use TalentLens as part of their recruitment process. Our processing relies on a lawful basis under data protection law. The Hiring Organisation determines the specific lawful basis for the recruitment activity overall.

You have the right to request more information about the lawful basis from the Hiring Organisation directly.

---

## How long we keep your data

We keep different types of data for different periods:

- **Inference logs containing your CV content:** 30 days from the date your application was assessed, after which they are automatically deleted
- **Aggregate, non-identifiable usage metrics:** 12 months for service operation purposes
- **AI-generated assessment of your application:** retained by the Hiring Organisation under their own retention schedule; please consult their privacy notice for details

---

## Where we process your data

If you applied for a role with a Hiring Organisation in the European Union, the United Kingdom, or the European Economic Area, your data is processed exclusively in our European data centre (eu-west-1, located in Ireland).

For other regions, your data may be processed outside your country of residence. Where this applies, we have appropriate safeguards in place to protect your data.

---

## Your rights

Under data protection law, you have several rights regarding your personal data, including the right to:

- **Access** the personal data we hold about you
- **Rectify** inaccurate personal data
- **Erase** your personal data in certain circumstances
- **Restrict or object** to certain processing
- **Receive** a copy of your data in a portable format
- **Lodge a complaint** with a supervisory authority (in Ireland, the Data Protection Commission)

To exercise any of these rights, please contact the Hiring Organisation in the first instance, since they are the primary controller of your personal data in the recruitment context. You may also contact Novara directly using the details below; we will assist where we are able to do so as a processor.

You may also request a human review of any decision affecting you by contacting the Hiring Organisation.
