1. Purpose and Scope
This policy establishes Novara AI’s mandatory standards for the responsible development, deployment,
and ongoing management of artificial intelligence features and products. It applies to all employees,
contractors, and third-party partners who design, build, train, evaluate, or operate AI-powered systems on
behalf of Novara AI.
The policy covers three interconnected domains:
• AI Product Development — model selection, training data practices, evaluation, and launch
criteria
• Privacy & Data Governance — personal data use in AI pipelines, consent, retention, and cross-
border transfer
• AI Governance & Risk Management — oversight structures, incident response, external audits,
and regulatory compliance
Out of Scope
Internal IT infrastructure not involving AI inference or training (covered by IT Security Policy NAI-POL-0018), and
academic research conducted under a separate IRB-approved protocol.
2. Definitions
The following terms have specific meanings throughout this document:
AI System Any software component that uses machine learning, large language models,
computer vision, or similar statistical techniques to generate outputs,
recommendations, or automated decisions.
Foundation Model A large pre-trained model (e.g., a transformer-based LLM or multimodal model)
obtained from a third-party provider or trained internally and used as a base for fine-
tuning or inference.
High-Risk AI Feature An AI System that makes or materially influences decisions affecting employment,
credit, healthcare, access to services, or legal rights, as further defined in Appendix
A.
Training Data Any dataset used to train, fine-tune, or conduct RLHF/preference-tuning on an AI
System, including data sourced from customers, third parties, or Novara AI’s own
infrastructure.
Personal Data Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person, as defined
under GDPR Art. 4(1), UK GDPR, CCPA, and equivalent applicable law.
Red-Teaming Structured adversarial testing conducted to identify failure modes, safety
vulnerabilities, and bias in an AI System prior to production deployment.
Shadow Mode A deployment configuration where an AI System generates outputs that are logged
and reviewed by humans but not surfaced to end users.
Model Card A standardised documentation artifact describing a model’s intended use cases,
evaluation metrics, limitations, and known risks.

3. AI Product Development Standards
3.1 Model Selection and Procurement
Prior to integrating any foundation model or third-party AI service, the responsible Product and
Engineering team must complete a Model Intake Assessment (template: NAI-TMPL-0031) covering the
following:
1. Licensing and intellectual property — confirm the model licence permits commercial use, fine-
tuning, and output commercialisation without royalty obligations.
2. Data provenance — obtain the provider’s data lineage documentation and assess for known
copyright, consent, or PII ingestion issues.
3. Security posture — review SOC 2 Type II report (or equivalent) and confirm the provider’s model
weights and inference endpoints are covered by an active Business Associate Agreement where
applicable.
4. Export controls — verify that deployment to Novara AI’s target geographies does not violate U.S.
Export Administration Regulations (EAR) or equivalent controls.
5. Performance benchmarks — document accuracy, latency (p50/p99), cost-per-token, and context
window on the relevant benchmark suite for the intended task.
The Chief AI Officer (CAIO) must counter-sign Model Intake Assessments for any foundation model that
will be used in a High-Risk AI Feature. The CAIO may delegate this authority in writing to the VP of AI
Engineering for non-high-risk use cases.
3.2 Training Data Governance
All training datasets — whether sourced internally, from customers, or from third-party data providers —
must be registered in Novara AI’s centralised Data Catalogue (Collibra instance) prior to use. Registration
requires:
• Source and collection method
• Legal basis for use (contract, licence, public domain, or legitimate interest assessment)
• Personal data classification (none / pseudonymised / identified) and applicable retention limit
• Known demographic or geographic skews that may introduce bias
• Data quality score (completeness, consistency, recency) assigned by the Data Engineering team
Prohibited Training Data Sources
Customer content may not be used as training data unless the customer has explicitly opted in via a signed Data
Processing Addendum that includes an AI Training Exhibit. Opt-in consent must be recorded in Salesforce CRM
and linked from the Data Catalogue entry. Retroactive use of customer data collected before an opt-in was
obtained is prohibited regardless of any current contractual status.
Synthetic data generated for training purposes must be documented with the generation methodology,
seed prompts (if LLM-derived), and any filtering steps applied. Synthetic data that mimics real customer
transactions or communications requires prior approval from the Data Privacy Officer (DPO).

3.4 Red-Teaming and Safety Testing
Every AI System must undergo a structured red-team exercise before Gate G2. Red-teaming must be
conducted by a team that did not build the model under review. For High-Risk AI Features, at least one
red-teamer must be external to Novara AI (a contracted third-party security or AI safety firm).
Red-team scope must include, at minimum:
• Prompt injection and jailbreak resistance
• Generation of harmful, illegal, or defamatory content
• Demographic bias and discriminatory output across protected characteristics (race, gender, age,
disability, religion, national origin, sexual orientation)
• Hallucination rate on factual queries relevant to the product domain
• PII leakage from training data or context windows
Red-team findings must be triaged using Novara AI’s AI Risk Severity Matrix (NAI-DOC-0017). All P0
(Critical) and P1 (High) findings must be resolved or formally risk-accepted by the CAIO before Gate G2
approval is granted.
3.5 Human-in-the-Loop Requirements
AI Systems that produce outputs that are directly acted upon by customers or that influence
consequential decisions must maintain a meaningful human-in-the-loop (HITL) mechanism. HITL
implementations are classified as follows:

• Level 1 — Mandatory Human Review: All AI outputs are reviewed and approved by a qualified
human operator before delivery. Required for High-Risk AI Features in healthcare, legal, and
financial verticals.
• Level 2 — Sampled Review: A statistically significant random sample (minimum 5% or 500
outputs per week, whichever is greater) is reviewed. Required for all other High-Risk AI Features.
• Level 3 — Exception-Based Review: Outputs flagged by automated confidence scoring or policy
filters are escalated for human review. Permitted for standard AI Features with documented
justification.
The applicable HITL level must be specified in the product’s Model Card and confirmed at Gate G2.
Downgrading the HITL level post-launch requires CAIO approval and a DPIA update.

4. Privacy and Data Protection
4.1 Legal Basis and Purpose Limitation
Novara AI processes personal data in AI pipelines only where a valid legal basis exists under the
applicable data protection law for each processing jurisdiction. Acceptable bases are:
6. Contract performance — where processing is strictly necessary to deliver a contracted service
feature.
7. Legitimate interest — where Novara AI has conducted and documented a Legitimate Interest
Assessment (LIA) confirming that its interests are not overridden by data subject rights.
8. Explicit consent — required for any use of personal data in model training or for sharing inference
outputs with third parties not covered by the original service agreement.
Personal data collected for one purpose may not be repurposed for AI training or evaluation without a
separate legal basis assessment. Product Managers must document purpose scope in the DPIA at
project inception.
4.2 Data Minimisation and Pseudonymisation
AI pipelines must be designed to minimise personal data ingestion. Specifically:
• Inference requests must not log raw personal data beyond the minimum retention period
necessary for debugging and safety review (default: 30 days; see Section 4.4).
• Training datasets containing personal data must be pseudonymised before use. Direct identifiers
(name, email, national ID, IP address) must be removed or tokenised using a reversible key
stored in a separate access-controlled vault.
• Where pseudonymisation is technically not feasible (e.g., face-recognition training datasets),
explicit consent and a DPIA signed by the DPO are required before ingestion.
• Embeddings generated from personal data are considered personal data and must be subject to
the same retention and deletion controls as the source data.
4.3 Data Subject Rights in AI Contexts
Novara AI’s standard data subject rights procedures (NAI-POL-0009) apply to all AI-processed personal
data. In addition, the following AI-specific obligations apply:
Right to Explanation Where an AI System makes or significantly influences a decision affecting a data
subject, the subject may request a plain-language explanation of the factors that
influenced the output. Product teams must implement an explanation endpoint
within 90 days of GA launch.
Right to Object to Data subjects may opt out of AI-driven profiling that produces legal or similarly
Automated Profiling significant effects. Product teams must implement a documented opt-out
mechanism and ensure the flag is propagated to all downstream inference pipelines
within 72 hours.
Right to Erasure Deletion requests must cascade to: (a) production inference logs, (b) fine-tuning
datasets containing the subject’s data, and (c) embedding stores. Where technical
constraints prevent deletion from a fine-tuned model, machine unlearning

4.5 Cross-Border Data Transfers
Where AI inference or training workloads involve the transfer of personal data outside the European
Economic Area (EEA) or United Kingdom, the following transfer mechanisms must be in place before
data is transmitted:
• Adequacy decision (where applicable, e.g., UK-US data bridge for qualifying transfers)
• Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) — EU 2021/914 Module 2 (controller-to-processor) or
Module 3 (processor-to-processor) as appropriate
• Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) completed and on file
Novara AI’s primary AI inference regions are us-east-1 (N. Virginia) and eu-west-1 (Ireland). Customer
data classified as EEA-residency-required must be processed exclusively in eu-west-1. This restriction
must be enforced at the infrastructure level via data residency tags in Terraform and verified quarterly by
the Infrastructure Security team.

5.3 Incident Response
AI-related incidents are managed within Novara AI’s standard incident management process (NAI-POL-
0021) with the following AI-specific additions:
Severity Definitions
• P0 (Critical): AI System produces outputs that cause or risk causing material harm to users or
third parties; data breach involving AI-processed personal data; systemic bias causing
discriminatory impact on a protected class.
• P1 (High): Significant hallucination rate increase detected in production; single high-severity
harmful output incident; HITL bypass discovered.
• P2 (Medium): Localised accuracy degradation below SLA thresholds; minor bias patterns
identified in monitoring; customer complaint regarding AI explainability.
• P3 (Low): Documentation gaps; minor policy non-compliance without user impact; near-miss
events.
Response Timelines
• P0: CAIO notified within 15 minutes of detection; AI System suspended or placed in Level 1 HITL
mode within 1 hour; DPO notified within 2 hours; preliminary incident report filed within 24 hours;
regulatory notification (where required) within 72 hours.
• P1: CAIO notified within 2 hours; remediation plan within 24 hours; full post-mortem within 5
business days.
• P2: Assigned to AI Safety Review Board within 24 hours; resolution within 14 days.
• P3: Logged in AI Risk Register; reviewed at next bi-weekly AI Safety Review Board meeting.
Post-Incident Review
All P0 and P1 incidents require a blameless post-mortem within 5 business days of resolution. Post-
mortems must be reviewed by the AI Ethics & Safety Committee and archived in Confluence (space: AI-
SAFETY). Recurring patterns across post-mortems must be escalated to the CAIO for systemic
remediation.

6. Responsible AI Principles
All AI development at Novara AI must be guided by the following principles, which are operationalised
through the standards in Sections 3–5:
• Beneficence — AI Systems must be designed to deliver measurable value to users and must not
be optimised for engagement at the expense of user wellbeing.
• Non-maleficence — Foreseeable harms must be systematically identified, mitigated, and
accepted only at a risk level explicitly approved by the CAIO.
• Fairness — AI Systems must not produce discriminatory outputs. Bias audits are mandatory, and
demographic parity, equalised odds, or other appropriate fairness metrics must be monitored
continuously in production.
• Transparency — Users must be informed when they are interacting with AI-generated content or
AI-influenced decisions. Model cards must be maintained and made available to enterprise
customers upon request.
• Accountability — Every AI System in production must have a named accountable owner.
Ownership must transfer when personnel change and must be reflected in the model registry
within 5 business days.
• Privacy by Design — Privacy considerations must be embedded from the earliest stages of AI
product design, not retrofitted after build.
• Human Oversight — Novara AI will not deploy AI Systems that are designed to operate without
any possibility of human intervention or override.

Requests to deviate from any mandatory requirement in this policy must be submitted via the AI Policy

• The specific policy clause for which an exception is sought
• Business justification and the intended duration of the exception

violation of applicable law.
9. Enforcement and Consequences
Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment or
contract, depending on severity and intent. Violations that result in harm to users or regulatory breaches
will be referred to Novara AI’s Legal team and, where required, reported to relevant regulatory authorities.
Employees who identify potential violations are encouraged to report them via Novara AI’s confidential
Ethics Hotline (ethics@novara.ai or the anonymous Vault platform). Retaliation against good-faith
reporters is strictly prohibited and constitutes a separate policy violation.

Appendix A — High-Risk AI Feature Criteria
An AI System is classified as a High-Risk AI Feature if it meets any of the following criteria:
9. The system’s outputs directly inform or substitute for a human decision that affects an individual’s
access to employment, promotion, or performance evaluation.
10. The system scores, ranks, or profiles individuals for credit, insurance, or financial product
eligibility.
11. The system generates clinical recommendations, diagnostic suggestions, or treatment plans in a
healthcare context.
12. The system is used by a customer in a regulated industry (financial services, healthcare, legal) to
produce outputs that are provided to end clients.
13. The system processes biometric data (facial recognition, voice identification, keystroke dynamics)
for authentication or identification.
14. The system is used to generate, curate, or rank content that will be seen by more than 1 million
users per month (large-scale recommender systems).
15. Legal or Compliance determines that the system falls within scope of the EU AI Act Annex III
high-risk categories.
Classification is determined at Gate G1 by the Product AI Champion in consultation with Legal and the
DPO. Borderline cases are escalated to the CAIO.
