## 2. Description of Processing

### 2.1 What TalentLens does

TalentLens is an AI-powered CV-screening and candidate-ranking service. Enterprise HR teams (Customers) submit candidate CVs and a job description; the system returns structured assessments of skill alignment, identified strengths, identified gaps, and a relative ranking when multiple candidates are submitted. Outputs are delivered to the Customer's recruiter for human review.

### 2.2 Categories of personal data processed

| Data category | Source | Purpose | Retention |
|---|---|---|---|
| Candidate CV content (work history, education, skills, contact details) | Customer (uploaded via API or web interface) | CV assessment and ranking | 30 days post-assessment for inference logs containing PII; per NAI-POL-0042 §4.4 |
| Job description text | Customer | Inference input | Same as above |
| Inferred candidate skill set | Generated by model | Customer assessment output | Same as above |
| Aggregate usage telemetry (no PII) | Service infrastructure | Service operation, billing, drift monitoring | 12 months (extendable to 24 for safety investigations) |

### 2.3 Scale of processing

- **Active enterprise customers:** 47 (as of February 2025)
- **Estimated candidate CVs assessed per month:** 250,000 across all customers
- **Estimated unique candidates per year:** 1.8–2.2 million
- **Geographic distribution of candidates:** ~70% EU/UK; ~20% North America; ~10% other (variable by customer)

### 2.4 Data flows

1. Customer's recruiter submits CV(s) and job description via TalentLens API or web UI
2. Request authenticated against Customer's tenant; routed to Novara inference cluster in eu-west-1 (Ireland) for EU-resident Customers, us-east-1 (N. Virginia) for North America Customers
3. CV content is preprocessed (PII redaction for inference; full content retained transiently for the inference call)
4. NovaraNet-HR-v2.3 produces structured assessment output
5. Output returned to Customer; raw inference log retained per retention schedule
6. Customer presents output (with interpretive guidance) to internal recruiter for HITL review per NAI-POL-0042 §3.5

### 2.5 Data subjects

The data subjects whose personal data is processed are:

- **Job candidates** (primary): individuals applying for roles at Customer organisations whose CVs are submitted to TalentLens for assessment
- **Recruiter users** (secondary): individuals at Customer organisations using the TalentLens interface; aggregate usage telemetry only

This DPIA focuses primarily on candidate data subjects; recruiter user data is covered under Novara's standard SaaS Data Processing Addendum.

## 3. Necessity and Proportionality

### 3.1 Why processing is necessary

Customer organisations face high volumes of candidate applications for advertised roles (typical enterprise role attracts 50–500 applications). Manual recruiter review is time-intensive and prone to recruiter fatigue and inconsistency. TalentLens enables consistent, structured first-pass assessment that supplements rather than replaces recruiter judgement.

### 3.2 Lawful basis

Under UK/EU GDPR Article 6(1)(f) (legitimate interests), Novara processes candidate data on behalf of Customers under their lawful basis for the recruitment activity. Customers are responsible for establishing their own lawful basis with respect to candidate data subjects. Novara as processor does not establish a separate lawful basis for candidate data processing; under our DPA Customers warrant they have the lawful basis to instruct Novara to process candidate data.

For special category data (Article 9), where present in CV content, lawful basis falls to the Customer; Novara does not knowingly process special category data and does not seek to do so.

### 3.3 Proportionality assessment

The processing is targeted and proportionate to the recruitment purpose: only data submitted by the Customer is processed; processing scope is limited to skill-matching and ranking against the supplied job description; outputs are structured and decision-support-only. Less invasive alternatives considered include unstructured AI-powered search (rejected as less informative) and pure keyword matching (rejected as less effective at semantic skill-matching).

## 4. Risks Identified

The following risks to data subjects (candidates) have been identified:

| Risk ID | Risk description | Severity (1–4) | Likelihood (1–4) | Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| R-01 | Candidate ranked unfavourably due to model bias against protected characteristics | 4 | 3 | 12 |
| R-02 | Unauthorised disclosure of CV content to a third party (data breach) | 4 | 1 | 4 |
| R-03 | False rejection of a qualified candidate due to model inaccuracy | 3 | 3 | 9 |
| R-04 | Candidate's CV content used for AI training without their consent | 4 | 1 | 4 |
| R-05 | Special category data (e.g., disability, health) inadvertently extracted from CV and used in ranking | 4 | 2 | 8 |
| R-06 | Candidate cannot effectively exercise UK GDPR rights against TalentLens-mediated decisions | 3 | 3 | 9 |
| R-07 | Cross-border transfer of EU candidate data to non-EU processing region | 3 | 2 | 6 |
| R-08 | Excessive retention of inference logs containing PII | 2 | 2 | 4 |
| R-09 | Misuse of TalentLens output by Customer for out-of-scope purposes (e.g., termination decisions) | 3 | 2 | 6 |
| R-10 | Output unavailability or service degradation affecting recruitment timelines (operational, not a data protection risk per se but covered for completeness) | 1 | 2 | 2 |

Risk scoring follows Novara's 4×4 likelihood-impact matrix per NAI-POL-0042 §5.2.

## 5. Mitigations

### 5.1 Mitigations applied per risk

| Risk ID | Mitigation | Owner | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| R-01 | Bias auditing methodology (NAI-DOC-0027) applied at each version release; demographic parity ≥ 0.85 required | AI Safety Review Board | Implemented; ongoing |
| R-01 | Demographic-balanced sampling during fine-tuning; counterfactual data augmentation | AI Engineering | Implemented in v2.3 |
| R-02 | TLS 1.3 encryption in transit; AES-256 at rest; SOC 2 Type II–audited security controls | Infrastructure Security | Implemented |
| R-02 | Tenant isolation at storage and inference layers | Infrastructure Security | Implemented |
| R-03 | Mandatory HITL Level 2 (sampled review of 5% or 500 outputs/week) | Customer (contractual obligation) | Customer-side responsibility |
| R-03 | Output confidence scoring; outputs below confidence threshold are flagged | AI Engineering | Implemented |
| R-04 | Customer DPA includes warranty that customer-supplied CVs may not be used for AI training without explicit Data Processing Addendum opt-in | Legal | Implemented |
| R-05 | TalentLens does not intentionally process special category data; recruiters trained not to upload CVs containing biometric or health data | Customer (contractual) | Customer-side responsibility |
| R-06 | Human review available on request via Customer | Customer | Customer-side responsibility |
| R-07 | EU-resident customer data routed exclusively to eu-west-1 region | Infrastructure Security | Implemented |
| R-07 | EU-to-non-EU transfers covered by Standard Contractual Clauses (EU 2021/914 Module 2) | Legal | Implemented |
| R-08 | 30-day retention for inference logs containing PII (NAI-POL-0042 §4.4); automated deletion | Data Engineering | Implemented |
| R-09 | TalentLens Acceptable Use Policy (NAI-POL-0033) prohibits out-of-scope use; contractual remedy for breach | Legal | Implemented |

### 5.2 Mitigation effectiveness assessment

The mitigations applied collectively reduce R-01 from initial score 12 to estimated residual score 6, R-03 from 9 to 4, R-05 from 8 to 4, R-06 from 9 to 6. R-04 is reduced to 1 by the contractual warranty. R-07 is reduced to 2 by SCC coverage and regional routing.

### 5.3 Residual risks

Residual risks accepted by the AI Ethics & Safety Committee are:

- **R-01 residual:** Some bias against under-represented demographic groups may persist beyond the measured threshold; this is monitored quarterly. Acceptable if quarterly demographic-parity audit shows scores ≥ 0.85.
- **R-03 residual:** Some false rejections will occur; the HITL Level 2 mechanism is the primary safeguard. Acceptable.
- **R-06 residual:** Customers vary in their operational implementation of human-review processes for candidate-initiated UK GDPR requests. Acceptable contingent on Customer due diligence.

No residual risk is rated above acceptable threshold per NAI-POL-0042 §5.2.
